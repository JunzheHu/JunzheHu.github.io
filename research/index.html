    Research¶

    Biorob Lab - EPFL¶

    Research Assistant, 01/2023 - 09/2023

    Project: Remote Control and Haptic Feedback in a 2 DOF Device for Thrombectomy Guidewire Navigation

    • Proposed a controller to achieve precise control of the translation and rotation of the guidewire on slave robot (with an average error of 0.31%) and gravity compensation for the center of gravity offset of the drive structure.

    • Built a Multi-threaded communication system based on Qt: The output variables of the master robot are used as input variables for the slave robotâ€™s position control of the guide wire (rotation angle, translation distance) and speed control; the proportional relationship between the master and slave robot variables is adjustable. The operation average delay is 0.091s.

    • At the slave robot, an exponentially weighted recursive least squares (EWRLS) algorithm estimates the respective parameters of the Kelvin-Voigt (KV) and Hunt-Crossley (HC) force models. The master robot then provides force feedback by interacting with a virtual environment via the estimated parameters.

    SIAT-CUHK Joint Laboratory of Precision Engineering - Chinese Academy of Science & CUHK¶

    Research Assistant (Paid), 09/2021 - 09/2022

    Project 1: Underwater Robotic with Flexible Passive Joint Paddle

    • Designed a multi-flexible articulated paddle as a propeller for swimming robots. The asymmetric variation of the structure was used to generate net thrust during the power stroke and recovery stroke.

    • Constructed and simulated a dynamics model based on the Pseudo-Rigid Body Model, Lagrangian Method and Blade Element Theory to reflect the motion and hydrodynamic force of the flexible jointed paddle.

    • A data-driven model combining hydrodynamic coefficients as well as SVD is proposed to reduce the total error between experimental and simulated average thrust data to 0.51% within the controller parameters, a reduction of 42.63 times.

    • By optimizing the control parameters and structure, the robot swims 3.3 times faster than the baseline, the yaw angle is 89.99% lower than the pre-transfer optimal control parameters.

    Project 2: Design and Anal Fin Optimization of Robotic Fish with Variable Stiffness

    • Designed a wire-driven robotic fish with variable stiffness body in BCF swimming form.

    • Introduced anal fins and coordination control (CPG) between the caudal and anal fins. This reduced head shaking by 65% compared to the robotic fish with no anal fin, or by 57% compared to that with a stationary anal fin.

    • Tested the prototype in the Chinese University of Hong Kong's reservoir to evaluate real-world performance.

    State Key Laboratory of Mechanical Transmissions - Chongqing University¶

    Research Assistant, 07/2021 - 12/2020

    Project: Sloth-inspired UAV Perching Climbing Grasping Mechanism

    • Proposed an adaptable UAV perching climbing grasping mechanism (UPCGM) from the structure and movement of sloths. The main innovation lies in the design of a step foot mechanism (SFM) consists of four step foot for perching, climbing, and grasping.

    • Designed series of simulation experiments on the basis of the initial machine model , and using genetic algorithm complete the optimal design of the whole machine integration of the flying robot. Adjusted the length of the step actuator and the distance between the step feet, and optimized the curve shape of the end toe mechanism.

    • The attachment experiment with the prototype proves that the flying robot has the ability to adapt to the attachment objects of different angles and sizes. The power consumption of attachment monitoring is only 4W (5% of the power consumption of flight monitoring). The continuous monitoring operation time of the flying robot can be increased to 5-12 hours.
